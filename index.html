How it works

Three steps to a lighter inbox

Open any email with attachments — the add-on sidebar appears automatically.

1

Open an email

Click any Gmail email with attachments. The Attachment Remover sidebar loads automatically alongside it.

2

Select & set options

Tick individual attachments or hit "Remove ALL". Choose whether to trash the original and set a custom label — all in the sidebar.

3

Done — same thread

A cleaned copy is inserted into the exact same thread with the original sender, timestamp, and body intact. Original is trashed or kept — your call.

FAQ

Questions we get a lot

Does it actually send the email to anyone?
No. We update your mailbox directly—it's like copying a file into a folder. Nobody in To, Cc, or Bcc receives anything or gets notified.
What happens to the original email?
That's up to you. A checkbox in the sidebar — checked by default — moves the original to Trash after the cleaned copy is created. Uncheck it to keep both in your inbox simultaneously.
Can I remove just some attachments, not all?
Yes. Use the checkboxes to select only the heavy files you want removed. Remaining attachments stay on the cleaned copy exactly as they were.
Can I use a custom label instead of the default one?
Yes. There's a label input field in the Options section. Leave it blank to use the default "Attachments Removed" label, type any name to create a custom label, or type none to skip labelling entirely. Gmail creates the label automatically if it doesn't exist.
Does uninstalling reset my free quota?
No. Your usage is linked directly to your Google account securely. Reinstalling does not reset the counter.
Is my email data stored anywhere?
Never. The add-on runs entirely within your Google account. No email content, metadata, or attachments are sent to or stored on our servers.
What Gmail permissions does it need and why?
We only request the permissions necessary to do the job: managing your labels, moving original emails to the trash, and securely updating your inbox with the clean copies.
Will inline images be removed?
No. Inline images embedded directly in the email body are never touched — only true file attachments listed in the sidebar are removable.
